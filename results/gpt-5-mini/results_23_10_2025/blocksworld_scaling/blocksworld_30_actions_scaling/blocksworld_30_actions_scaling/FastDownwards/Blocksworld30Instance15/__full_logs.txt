PROBLEM
------------------------------
Two agents manipulate labelled blocks on a table following standard BlocksWorld rules: only the top block of a stack can be moved and only one block can be carried at a time. The blocks involved are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K. The initial arrangement is: A is on top of J, B is on the table, C is on top of G, D is on top of B, E is on top of F, F is on top of D, G is on the table, H is on top of E, I is on the table, J is on top of K, K is on the table. The agents then execute the following actions in order:
1. unstack(H, E)
2. putdown(H)
3. pickup(H)
4. stack(H, E)
5. unstack(A, J)
6. putdown(A)
7. pickup(A)
8. stack(A, H)
9. pickup(I)
10. putdown(I)
11. unstack(A, H)
12. putdown(A)
13. pickup(I)
14. stack(I, A)
15. unstack(I, A)
16. stack(I, A)
17. unstack(H, E)
18. stack(H, C)
19. unstack(E, F)
20. stack(E, J)
21. unstack(H, C)
22. stack(H, I)
23. unstack(H, I)
24. stack(H, C)
25. unstack(F, D)
26. stack(F, D)
27. unstack(H, C)
28. stack(H, F)
29. unstack(I, A)
30. stack(I, C)
Using the initial configuration and the actions, derive the final arrangement of the blocks. State for each block whether it is on the table or on top of another block.
